01 · Brand & logo

One identity, two moods.

The supplied logo is a navy-and-gold financial mark — formal, charted, slightly traditional. The live site is the opposite: white paper, near-black ink, and saturated chartreuse / pink panels. We carry both palettes: the website's energy as the primary system, the logo's navy / gold as a secondary register for serious contexts (audits, proposals, reports).

Replace the AI illustrations.

The current site uses Gemini-generated 3D cartoons and a flock of unrelated SVG icons (tap, pay, go) that have nothing to do with cloud consulting. This is the weakest part of the existing system. Refined approach below.

Hand-drawn-feel architecture diagrams. Strictly two-color: ink lines, chartreuse highlight on the part being discussed.

Option B · Photographic
photo · berlin · ws.placeholder

Berlin-textured photography. Brutalist concrete, server rooms, whiteboards. Desaturated, ink-graded.

Option C · Numbered chips
01 AUDIT 02 BLUEPRINT 03 BUILD 04 HAND-OFF

No imagery at all — let typography & numbered chips carry the page. Most disciplined option.

Recommendation: default to Option C for service pages (no imagery), use Option A diagrams sparingly for one hero illustration per page. Drop the Gemini cartoons entirely.

10 · Copy voice

Architect, not vendor.

The site already has a strong voice: short imperatives, no jargon-stuffing, German-engineering directness with a Berlin-startup edge. "Stop reacting. Start architecting." is the model line.

↑ Yes

  • Short, declarative sentences. Verbs first.
  • Imperatives: "Stop reacting." "Schedule a session." "Architect for scale."
  • Specific words: AWS, microservices, CI/CD, technical debt, audit.
  • Outcomes, not features: "weeks, not quarters" beats "fast turnaround".
  • One claim per line. Whitespace does the rest.

↓ No

  • "Synergy", "leverage", "unlock", "empower" (already in hero — fix it).
  • Three-adjective stacks: "robust, scalable, maintainable" — pick one.
  • Emoji and exclamation marks. Ever.
  • "We help you" framing. Be the architect, not the helper.
  • Stock tech metaphors (rocket ships, journeys, ecosystems).
Sample linesreuse / remix

12 · Refinement notes

What I cleaned up.

You asked for "same vibe, refined where weak." Here is what I changed and why — so the future landing pages can deviate intentionally.

  1. Logo / palette tension. The live site (chartreuse + pink) doesn't match the supplied logo (navy + gold). I made chartreuse/pink the primary energy and demoted navy/gold to a secondary register, because the site's color is what's actually working.
  2. Imagery. The site's Gemini-generated 3D cartoons feel temporary and undermine the senior-consultant positioning. Replaced with three disciplined options (line diagrams, photography, no imagery).
  3. Random icon flock. The "tap-pay-go" SVGs in the CTA section appear to be stock leftovers unrelated to consulting. Removed entirely.
  4. Type scale. Hero on the live site is around 56px; bumped to a confident 88px display. Body sized up to 17px from ~15px for readability.
  5. Button shape. Standardized on a 6px-radius rectangle with a 1.5px ink border (the live site mixes radii and border weights).
  6. Typo: "Conatct". Live site footer button reads "Conatct" — flagged for fix.
  7. Hero copy. Live subheading ends with a stray "1": "…and Technology Strategy.1". Cleaned in the system examples.
  8. Voice. Replaced "Empowering businesses with expert…" hero with the imperative model already used elsewhere on the site ("Stop reacting…"). Consistency.
